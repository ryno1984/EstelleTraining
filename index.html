Medical Safety First

It is strongly advised that you clear this plan with a trained healthcare professional before starting. This plan is structured around your specific medical history and current movement limitations using evidence-informed guidance from recognized public health and rehabilitation organizations.
  • Stop immediately for chest pain, faintness, severe breathlessness, severe palpitations, new neurological symptoms, or sharp joint pain.
  • Do not train on days with acute illness/fever, uncontrolled blood pressure, or unusual fatigue/pain spike.
  • Avoid breath-holding/straining. Keep breathing continuous during every rep.

Effort Rules (Simple)

  • RPE target: stay between 2 and 4 out of 10 for almost all work.
  • Pain target: stay between 0 and 3 out of 10 during and after training.
  • Breathing rule: if you cannot speak a full sentence, effort is too high for this phase.
  • Upper-body rule: no overhead lifting and no loaded end-range shoulder flexion in this phase.
  • Load rule: start upper-body dumbbells at 1kg. Progress only after 2 pain-free weeks.
What is RPE (effort out of 10)?
0 = complete rest, 1-2 = very easy, 3-4 = easy to moderate, 5-6 = hard, 7-8 = very hard, 9-10 = near maximal. For this plan, stay mostly at RPE 2-4.
How to score pain out of 10:
0 = no pain, 1-2 = mild awareness, 3 = noticeable but manageable and settles quickly, 4 = too much for this plan (reduce range/reps now), 5+ or sharp pain = stop exercise.
Minimum win is consistency. A 10-minute safe session is a successful session.

Meal Plan (3-Week Rotation)

Low-budget, lower-carb, gallbladder-safe portions; protein at every meal; easy swaps. Choose the week that matches your energy and stick to it for 7 days.

Portions (per meal, typical): chicken/thigh/white fish 120–150 g cooked; canned tuna/sardines 1 can (≈120 g drained); eggs 2 whole; beans/lentils/chickpeas ½–1 cup cooked; oats ½ cup dry; yogurt ¾–1 cup (plain/Greek); olive/canola oil 1–2 tsp; nuts/peanut butter 1 Tbsp.

Week 1 – Settle & Light

  • Breakfast options: 2 eggs + tomato/cucumber + 1 tsp olive oil; or ½ cup oats cooked in water + 1 scoop whey or 2 egg whites stirred in + cinnamon + ½ apple.
  • Lunch options: 1 can tuna in water + ½–1 cup mixed beans + 1–2 cups frozen veg + 1 tsp olive/canola oil + lemon/pepper; or lentil soup (1 cup cooked lentils) with side salad.
  • Snack (pick 1–2): ¾ cup plain/Greek yogurt + cinnamon; or 1 apple/pear + 1 Tbsp peanut butter; or 20–30 g peanuts/almonds.
  • Dinner options: 120–150 g baked white fish + 2 cups roasted/steam veg + ½ cup chickpeas; or egg scramble (2 whole + 2 whites) with spinach/onion/tomato + side salad.
  • Starch placement: keep starch small (oats or ½ cup beans/chickpeas) and near earlier meals; skip rice/potato this week.
  • Hydration: 300–400 ml water each meal + 2–3 mugs unsweetened tea; max 2 coffees before 2 pm.

Week 2 – Moderate Carbs Around Bike

  • Pre-ride (optional): ½ banana or ½ cup oats with cinnamon + 10–15 g protein (small yogurt or 1 boiled egg).
  • Breakfast options: Protein oats (½ cup oats + scoop whey) with ½ cup berries; or 2 eggs + 1 slice seed/wholegrain bread + tomato.
  • Lunch options: 120–150 g chicken thigh (skin off) stir-fry with 2 cups frozen veg + ½ cup brown rice; or tuna/bean bowl as Week 1.
  • Snack: yogurt or fruit + peanut butter; or 2 boiled eggs if hungrier.
  • Dinner options: Chickpea curry (1 cup chickpeas) over ½ cup brown rice or over steamed veg; or fish + veg + small potato (1 small, skin on) if ankle swelling is stable.
  • Fat cap per meal: 1–2 tsp oil; nuts/peanut butter 1 Tbsp max.

Week 3 – Slightly Leaner (Same Portions, Less Starch)

  • Breakfast: Rotate eggs + veg or protein oats; no bread this week.
  • Lunch: Tuna/bean bowl or chicken + veg; starch only if biking that day (½ cup rice/beans max).
  • Snack: yogurt or fruit + peanut butter; skip nuts on non-training days if weight loss stalls.
  • Dinner: Fish or egg scramble + big veg volume; chickpeas/beans ½ cup max; no potatoes/rice unless post-bike.
  • Hydration push: 3 L fluids target (water + tea); add pinch of salt + lemon to one bottle if low energy and BP is stable.

Quick Plate Builder (any week)

  • Protein: 120–150 g cooked meat/fish OR 2 eggs + 2 whites OR ¾–1 cup yogurt OR 1 cup beans/lentils.
  • Veg: 2 cups non-starchy veg (frozen is fine) – aim for 2 colors.
  • Starch (optional): ½ cup oats/rice/beans OR 1 small potato OR ½ banana near biking.
  • Fat: 1–2 tsp olive/canola oil OR 1 Tbsp peanut butter OR 20–30 g nuts (pick one).
Gallbladder removed: keep fats modest each meal; if you feel greasy stools or cramping, halve fat and increase veg/beans instead.

Prep Once, Eat All Week (budget)

  • Batch lentil or chickpea curry (4–5 portions) on Sunday.
  • Cook 1–1.5 kg chicken thighs in tray bake; freeze half.
  • Keep 6–8 boiled eggs ready for snacks/breakfast.
  • Use frozen mixed veg + frozen spinach to avoid waste.
  • Keep 4–6 cans tuna/sardines; rotate to avoid boredom.

Safety + Tracking

  • Log weight 1–2x/week, BP 3–4x/week, morning HR, energy 1–10.
  • If BP ~180/110 or higher, skip hard training and seek advice.
  • If meals cause gut upset, shrink fat portions first, then add veg/beans to keep fullness.
  • Minimum win: 3 balanced meals + 2.5 L water on any day.

Warm-up

Diaphragmatic Breathing (Supine or Seated)

1-2 rounds x 60-90 sec

Very Easy
  • Start: Lie on a mat with knees bent and feet flat, or sit with back supported.
  • Hand position: One hand on chest, one hand on lower belly.
  • Breathe in: Inhale through nose for about 4 seconds so belly rises first.
  • Breathe out: Exhale through mouth for about 6 seconds; keep jaw and shoulders relaxed.
  • Stop if dizzy: Pause. Roll to one side before sitting up so shoulders and neck stay supported.

Targets: Diaphragm, deep core support muscles, and nervous-system down-regulation.

Why this is in your plan: Breathing control lowers stress response and muscle guarding before movement.

How this helps you: Makes sessions feel safer, lowers tension, and improves tolerance on low-energy days.

Cat-Cow (Small Range)

1-2 sets x 6 slow reps

Back Mobility
  • Start: On a mat, place hands under shoulders and knees under hips (tabletop).
  • Round: Exhale and gently round upper/mid back. Tuck chin slightly.
  • Arch: Inhale and gently arch lower back while lifting chest slightly.
  • Tempo: Move slowly, pause 1 second in each position, for 6 controlled reps.
  • Safety + get up: Stop if shoulders hurt. To get up, sit hips toward heels, move to side-sit, then push up with forearm and hand.

Targets: Spinal mobility (thoracic and lumbar segments) and gentle trunk control.

Why this is in your plan: Slow segmental motion reduces stiffness without high joint load.

How this helps you: Helps loosen upper/lower back and prepares you for the rest of the session.

Main Work

Sit-to-Stand From High Chair

1-3 sets x 5-8 reps

Lower Body
  • Start: Sit near front of a high chair with feet hip-width and knees over ankles.
  • Stand: Lean chest slightly forward, press feet into floor, and stand up slowly.
  • Sit down: Reach hips back to chair and lower down slowly with control.
  • Breathe: Exhale on the way up, inhale on the way down.
  • Safety: Use hands on chair if needed. Stop for sharp knee or back pain.

Targets: Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, and core bracing muscles.

Why this is in your plan: This is a safe way to build leg strength for daily life patterns.

How this helps you: Improves getting up from chairs, stairs, and overall confidence with less back strain.

Video form check

Wall Push-Up

1-3 sets x 5-8 reps

Shoulder Safe
  • Start: Stand facing wall, hands at chest height, slightly wider than shoulders.
  • Lower: Inhale and bend elbows 30-45 degrees as chest moves toward wall.
  • Push: Exhale and press wall away to return to start.
  • Posture: Keep body in one line, no shrugging shoulders.
  • Safety: Stop for shoulder pinch, instability, or sharp pain. Step closer to wall to make it easier.

Targets: Chest, triceps, serratus, and shoulder stabilizers (rotator cuff support).

Why this is in your plan: Closed-chain pressing on a wall is lower risk than floor pressing for a sensitive shoulder.

How this helps you: Builds shoulder strength and control while minimizing re-injury risk.

Glute Bridge (Double Leg)

1-3 sets x 6-10 reps

Hip + Back Support
  • Start: Lie on mat, knees bent, feet flat, arms by your sides.
  • Lift: Exhale, tighten glutes, and lift hips to a comfortable height.
  • Hold: Pause 1-2 seconds while keeping ribs down.
  • Lower: Inhale and lower slowly, one smooth motion.
  • Safety + get up: If back pain or hamstring cramp appears, lower height. Roll to side to stand up safely.

Targets: Glutes, hamstrings, posterior chain, and pelvic stabilizers.

Why this is in your plan: Stronger glutes reduce overload on lumbar spine and hips.

How this helps you: Can decrease chronic low-back ache and improve hip stability while walking.

Supported March In Place

1-3 sets x 20-40 sec

Gentle Cardio

  • Move: Lift one knee a little, place it down, then switch sides.
  • Tempo: Keep it slow and even, no bouncing.
  • Breathe: Normal breathing; stay at easy talk pace.
  • Safety: Stop and sit if dizzy, chest symptoms, or unusual palpitations appear.

Targets: Hip flexors, lower core, calf pump, and low-level cardio system.

Why this is in your plan: Very gentle cardiovascular work improves circulation without impact.

How this helps you: Builds baseline conditioning and energy tolerance with minimal joint stress.

Open Book (Thoracic Rotation)

1-2 sets x 4-6 each side

Back Mobility
  • Start: Lie on side with hips and knees bent, knees stacked.
  • Set position: Keep both knees touching to protect lower back.
  • Move: Inhale and slowly open top arm and chest toward the other side.
  • Return: Exhale and bring arm back to start with control.
  • Safety + get up: Stop before shoulder pain. Roll to side, then push up with forearm and hand.

Targets: Thoracic mobility, chest tissue opening, and upper-back rotation control.

Why this is in your plan: Improving thoracic movement reduces compensation through lower back and shoulder.

How this helps you: Can ease upper-back stiffness and make shoulder movement feel less restricted.

Video form check

Band Row (Elbows Close)

1-3 sets x 6-10 reps

Shoulder Safe
  • Start: Use the long band anchored at chest height; stand tall with soft knees.
  • Pull: Exhale and draw elbows back close to your ribs.
  • Pause: Hold 1 second, gently squeeze shoulder blades.
  • Return: Inhale and slowly extend arms back to start.
  • Safety: Keep elbow close to body, no jerking. Stop for shoulder pinch, tendon pain, or instability.

Targets: Mid-back (rhomboids/traps), lats, rear shoulder, scapular stabilizers.

Why this is in your plan: Postural pulling strength protects neck/shoulders and supports shoulder mechanics.

How this helps you: Improves shoulder stability and tolerance for daily pulling/lifting tasks.

Video form check

Dead Bug Heel Tap (Modified)

1-3 sets x 4-6 each side

Core Control
  • Start: Lie on back with knees bent at 90 degrees and arms up.
  • Brace: Gently flatten lower back into the mat before each rep.
  • Move: Exhale and lower one heel to tap floor, then return and switch sides.
  • Control: Slow reps only; keep ribs down.
  • Safety + get up: If lower back arches or hurts, shorten range. Roll to side to get up.

Targets: Deep core (transverse abdominis), obliques, and trunk-limb coordination.

Why this is in your plan: Teaches spinal control while arms/legs move, which is back-protective.

How this helps you: Can reduce back pain spikes during normal movement and improve core confidence.

Video form check

Seated Pallof Press (Band)

1-3 sets x 6-8 presses each side

Core Anti-Rotation
  • Start: Sit tall on a chair, feet flat. Anchor long band at chest height to the side; hold band at chest with both hands.
  • Press: Exhale and slowly press arms straight forward without letting trunk rotate.
  • Return: Inhale and bring hands back to chest with control.
  • Switch sides: Turn chair so band pulls from the other side and repeat.
  • Safety: Keep arms below shoulder height, grip light, and stop if shoulder/bicep discomfort appears.

Targets: Deep core (anti-rotation), obliques, and scapular stabilizers with minimal shoulder load.

Why this is in your plan: Builds trunk stability without weight-bearing through the ankle or heavy shoulder effort.

How this helps you: Improves balance and back protection for daily tasks and cycling posture.

Hip Hinge To Wall

1-3 sets x 6-8 reps

Hip Patterning
  • Start: Stand 15-20 cm from wall with back facing wall, hands on hips.
  • Move back: Push hips backward until glutes touch wall lightly.
  • Return: Press feet into floor and stand tall again.
  • Posture: Keep chest open, knees soft, and spine neutral.
  • Safety: Stop if lower-back pain increases. Keep range small and controlled.

Targets: Glutes, hamstrings, and safe hip-dominant movement patterning.

Why this is in your plan: Re-trains bending mechanics so load goes to hips, not low back.

How this helps you: Makes daily bending safer and can reduce pain from poor movement strategy.

Video form check

Calf Raise (Supported)

1-3 sets x 8-12 reps

Lower Limb Endurance
  • Start: Stand tall, feet hip-width, fingers lightly on wall/chair.
  • Lift: Exhale and rise onto balls of feet for 2 seconds.
  • Hold: Pause 1 second at the top.
  • Lower: Inhale and lower heels for 2-3 seconds.
  • Safety: No bouncing. Stop for sharp Achilles/calf pain.

Targets: Calves, ankle stabilizers, and lower-limb endurance.

Why this is in your plan: Ankle/calf strength supports walking efficiency and circulation.

How this helps you: Improves walking tolerance and reduces early leg fatigue.

Video form check

Shoulder External Rotation Isometric

2-3 sets x 10-20 sec each side

Stability
  • Start: Stand with elbow at side, bent 90 degrees, small towel under elbow.
  • Press: Back of hand gently presses outward into wall or light long-band tension.
  • Effort: Use only 30-40% effort, not maximal force.
  • Breathe: Keep normal breathing through the 10-20 second hold.
  • Safety: Keep arm below shoulder height. Stop for sharp pain, popping, or pulling sensation.

Targets: Rotator cuff (especially external rotators) and shoulder joint stability.

Why this is in your plan: Low-load isometrics strengthen shoulder support without risky movement range.

How this helps you: Helps protect your previously injured shoulder and improve stability.

Seated Pelvic Tilt

1-2 sets x 8-10 reps

Back Relief
  • Start: Sit tall near front of chair, feet flat, hands on thighs.
  • Move 1: Exhale and gently tuck pelvis (lower back flattens slightly).
  • Move 2: Inhale and gently tip pelvis forward (small arch).
  • Tempo: Slow, small movement only, 8-10 reps.
  • Safety: Stop for shooting or radiating back/leg pain.

Targets: Deep core timing and gentle lumbar-pelvic motion control.

Why this is in your plan: Small controlled pelvic motion reduces protective muscle stiffness.

How this helps you: Can ease low-back stiffness and improve sitting tolerance.

Video form check

Low Step-Up (Supported)

1-3 sets x 4-8 each side

Leg Strength
  • Start: Use a very low step. Hold rail/chair support before moving.
  • Step up: Place full foot on step and press through heel to stand.
  • Step down: Lower slowly back to floor with control.
  • Alignment: Knee tracks over middle toes, not collapsing inward.
  • Safety: Stop for sharp knee or hip pain. Reduce step height if needed.

Targets: Quads, glutes, calves, and balance support muscles.

Why this is in your plan: This is progressive functional leg training in a controlled height/range.

How this helps you: Improves stair function, leg confidence, and general daily mobility.

Video form check

Supported Split-Stance Weight Shift

1-3 sets x 20-30 sec each side

Hip Mobility
  • Start: One foot forward, one foot back, both feet pointing ahead, hold support.
  • Shift forward: Move weight gently forward until mild stretch only.
  • Shift back: Return slowly to middle, then gently backward.
  • Range: Stay in controlled mid-range to protect hips and lower back.
  • Safety: Stop if hip pinch or back pain appears.

Targets: Hip mobility, glute medius control, and front-back weight transfer mechanics.

Why this is in your plan: Gradual weight shift restores hip motion without deep end-range loading.

How this helps you: Reduces hip stiffness and improves gait comfort.

Wall Bird-Dog (Standing)

1-3 sets x 4-6 each side

Core + Shoulder Control
  • Start: Hands on wall at shoulder height, step back slightly, soft knees.
  • Brace: Tighten core gently so trunk stays still.
  • Reach: Lift one arm and opposite leg in a small range, then return slowly.
  • Breathe: Exhale on reach, inhale on return.
  • Safety: Stop if shoulder or back pain appears. Keep movement smaller if unstable.

Targets: Core anti-rotation control, glute support, and shoulder stabilization.

Why this is in your plan: Builds whole-body coordination with low load and high control.

How this helps you: Supports back stability and shoulder control during daily tasks.

Side-Lying Clamshell (No End-Range)

1-3 sets x 8-12 each side

Hip Stability
  • Start: Lie on side with knees bent, heels together, hips stacked.
  • Move: Exhale and lift top knee halfway only (no forcing range). Use loop band above knees if easy.
  • Control: Pause 1 second, then lower slowly while inhaling.
  • Position: Keep pelvis steady; do not roll backward.
  • Safety + get up: Stop for hip pinch. Roll forward, use both hands and forearm to push up.

Targets: Glute medius/minimus and deep hip external rotators.

Why this is in your plan: Hip stabilizers control pelvis and reduce knee/back compensation.

How this helps you: Improves hip stability and may reduce lateral hip/low-back irritation.

Low-Energy Day Algorithm (Green / Yellow / Red)

Green Day: Normal symptoms. Do full planned session.
Yellow Day: More fatigue/pain than usual but still safe. Do 50% volume (half sets, slower tempo).
Red Day: Significant symptom spike or warning signs. Skip strength. Do only 3-5 min breathing + gentle mobility, then rest.

3-Minute Rescue Routine

1) Breathing Reset

60 sec

  • Start: Sit with back supported, both feet on floor, hands relaxed on thighs.
  • Breathe in: Inhale through nose for 4 seconds.
  • Breathe out: Exhale through mouth for 6 seconds (long and gentle).
  • Tempo: Repeat for 6-8 slow breaths (about 60 seconds).
  • Safety: Stop if dizzy, chest pain, or unusual breathlessness appears.

Targets: Breathing mechanics and autonomic calming response.

Why this is in your plan: This rapidly lowers arousal and helps symptom control during low-energy or bad-pain days.

How this helps you: Gives you a safe "reset" when energy or symptoms are unstable.

2) Seated Pelvic Tilt

6 reps

  • Start: Sit tall near front of chair, feet flat, hands on thighs.
  • Move 1: Exhale and gently tuck pelvis so low back flattens slightly.
  • Move 2: Inhale and gently tip pelvis forward into a tiny arch.
  • Tempo: 6 slow reps, no forcing range.
  • Safety: Stop for sharp pain, radiating pain, or numbness.

Targets: Gentle lumbar-pelvic mobility and low-level core activation.

Why this is in your plan: Keeps back moving with minimal load when full exercise is not possible.

How this helps you: Prevents stiffness from escalating on bad symptom days.

3) Gentle March Seated

45-60 sec

  • Start: Sit tall with back supported, hold chair sides if needed.
  • Move: Alternate lifting knees in a small range, one at a time.
  • Breathe: Keep normal breathing and relaxed shoulders.
  • Tempo: Slow and steady for 45-60 seconds.
  • Safety: Stop immediately if symptoms escalate.

Targets: Hip flexors, circulation, and very low-intensity cardio system.

Why this is in your plan: Maintains blood flow and movement without impact or shoulder load.

How this helps you: Supports conditioning and energy without provoking joints.

Stop + Seek Medical Review If

  • New chest symptoms, severe shortness of breath, or fainting sensation.
  • Joint swelling/hot joint worsening after sessions for more than 24-48 hours.
  • Back or shoulder pain sharply worsens and does not settle with load reduction.
  • Any symptom that feels different from your normal baseline pattern.

What To Track Each Session







Tracking Cadence (Keep It Consistent)

  • Daily: session done (yes/no), pain 0-10, energy 1-10, sleep quality 1-5.
  • 3-4x/week: resting HR and blood pressure.
  • Weekly: 5-minute easy bike test + body weight.
  • Every 2 weeks: waist measurement + progress photos (optional).
Use the same conditions every time (same time of day, same route, similar weather where possible) so your data is comparable.

Vitals and Objective Progress (Simple)

  • Resting heart rate (RHR): log in the morning, before caffeine, at least 4 days/week.
  • Body weight: log 1-2x/week, same time of day.
  • Waist measurement: log every 2 weeks.
  • Blood pressure (BP): log at least 3 days/week and before harder days.
  • Sleep: log hours slept + sleep quality (1-5).
  • Energy and pain: log daily energy (1-10) and pain (0-10).
Weekly 5-minute bike test (same day/time each week):
Warm up 2-3 minutes easy. Then do a 5-minute steady bike at talk pace in an easy gear (same bike/settings each week). Record: distance (or speed/total watts if available), average HR, peak HR, RPE, ankle/back/shoulder symptoms, and 10-minute recovery feeling.
BP safety reminder: if resting BP is around 180/110 or higher, skip training and seek medical advice. If around 180/120 with symptoms (chest pain, breathlessness, neuro symptoms), seek urgent care.

Expected Progress If Consistent

  • Weeks 1-2: learns form and tolerates short 10-20 minute sessions.
  • Weeks 3-6: less stiffness, slightly better daily energy, improved confidence.
  • Weeks 7-10: can handle longer sessions, better movement control and posture.
  • Weeks 11-12: stronger baseline and readiness for next progressive block.
Progress may still be non-linear with hormones and past injury history. Consistency and smart load progression matter more than perfect weeks.
